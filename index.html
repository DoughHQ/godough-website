Why Dough exists

You already know
what you love.

The grocery store
just makes it
hard to find.

Thousands of products. Endless decisions. Analysis paralysis in every aisle. Most of the time you reach for what you recognize — not necessarily what you'd actually love.

Dough gives you that moment of clarity.

There's more information in a single honest choice — between two products you genuinely know — than in everything you've ever put in a cart. That moment of real preference has always been there. Dough pays attention to it.

Your taste shifts by mood, by moment, by occasion. If we know what you choose when forced to choose, we know what you love. Not what's popular. What's genuinely, specifically yours.

Every choice teaches Dough something true. The things that keep winning find their way to your plate. The things that don't, don't.

The more you use it, the more it sounds like you.
